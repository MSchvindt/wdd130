What are hot springs?

Thermal waters are called mineral waters that come out of the ground with more than 5 Â°C than the surface temperature. These waters come from underground layers of the Earth that are at a higher temperature, which are rich in different mineral components and allow their therapeutic use (hydrotherapy) such as baths, inhalations, irrigation, and heating. They are usually found along fault lines, since groundwater can be introduced along the fault plane, which heats up when it reaches a certain depth and then rises in the form of steam (which can condense when it reaches the Surface, forming a geyser) or hot water.
